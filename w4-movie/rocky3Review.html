Review

Charles LaRose April 22, 2026


Rocky III takes a different approach from the first two films which I admire and enjoy. Rocky is no longer broke or a bum. In fact he lives in a mansion and has maids plus fancy sports cars. What is weird though is how in the first two films Rocky was almost illiterate as he could not read and speak clearly. A big part of Rocky II in fact was him being treated like a complete idiot by a director for a commercial when he couldn’t read dummy cards. Instead in this film we see in a montage him being a spokesperson for all kinds of products and even in an episode of the Muppets.

The whole theme around this film is being able to pick yourself backup after failure and always staying hungry. We see Rocky become civilized and not the rough and tough machine he once was. Eventually this leads to him losing a fight to the mean Clubber Lang. Rocky loses all his confidence and falls into deeper depression when his trainer dies prior to the fight with Clubber.

The film throws the best plot twist of the entire franchise though when the enemy from the first two films “Apollo Creed” offers to train Rocky for a rematch against Clubber. I love this twist because it’s very surprising to the viewer on the first watch and makes for an unpredictable story. Eventually the two become best friends and Rocky gets his confidence back. Leading to him winning his rematch against Clubber Lang.

I like how this entry in the film changes things up. It would have been very easy for the producers of the film to just follow the blueprint of the first two films with a conflict, training montage, and then a fight. However, having a second fight at the beginning where Rocky loses changes up the pacing of the film which I am a fan of.

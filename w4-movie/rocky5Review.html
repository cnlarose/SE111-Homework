Review

Charles LaRose April 22, 2026


Soon after Rocky seeks revenge and wants to fight Drago. Everyone tells him he’s basically insane for doing this but Rocky follows through anyway. I love the training montage in this film. Watching Rocky run through snowy mountains and pickup logs like they are weights is extremely iconic to this day. For my money this film also has the best music of the whole series as well as the best choreographed fight scene with Balboa and Drago. Many casual fans of the series will say this is their favorite film of the franchise. It is something that would never be recreated in today’s day and age. A true product of its time.

Rocky V is my favorite film of the series despite being a very controversial opinion. The film has flaws but also makes up for them with lots of changes to the series formula. One of the common criticisms of the film is the how somehow Robert Balboa ages 5-6 years while Rocky and Adrian were gone in Russia. This is due to Robert being played by a different actor (Sylvester Stallone’s son) but it’s very odd for the viewer seeing Rocky get off a plane from Russia and his son looks completely different. Another common criticism is the soundtrack aging terribly. The music is very late 80s/early 90s rap rather then the orchestra music we are used to from Bill Conti. Lastly, the film never has Rocky fight a boxing match. Instead, the film concludes with Rocky having a street fight against his protege Tommy Gunn.

Rocky trains Gunn and basically neglects his own son and family for him. The story of keeping your family close and family being the most important thing in the world really resonates with me. Watching Rocky try to reclaim glory by training Tommy is a very sad watch for the viewer. You can sense Rocky feels like he’s a nobody again and just wants his respect back. In the end Tommy gets tricked by a sleezy promoter into challenging Rocky to a fight. Rocky initially disagrees but after Tommy punches Paulie the fight is on. The film ends with a satisfying conclusion of Rocky beating Tommy.

Trivia

  • Tommy Morrison won 2 heavyweight championships as a professional boxer in real life.
  • The original script had Rocky Balboa dying in Adrian's arms after winning the street fight against Tommy.
  • Rocky Jr. is played by Sylvester Stallone's youngest son.
